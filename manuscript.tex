\section{Introduction}
We settle the foundational complexity question $P \neq NP$ by proving that the deterministic Boolean circuit size for the exact evaluation of the non-local Subset Sum Problem is exponentially bounded by $\Omega(2^{\gamma n})$.

\section{The Hypercube Entropy Boundary}
Let $f_n: \{0, 1\}^n \rightarrow \{0, 1\}$ be the indicator function of the Subset Sum Problem for a given set of weights $W = \{w_1, w_2, \dots, w_n\} \subset \mathbb{Z}$ and a target sum $T = 0$. The input vector $x = (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n) \in \{0, 1\}^n$ dictates the inclusion ($x_i = 1$) or exclusion ($x_i = 0$) of elements. The function evaluates as:

\begin{equation}
f_n(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i w_i = 0 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\end{equation}

Let $C$ be a deterministic Boolean circuit over the standard basis $B = \{\land, \lor, \neg\}$ that computes $f_n(x)$. A polynomial circuit $n^k$ cannot process the independent boundary states across the hypercube network without causing mathematical collision states. To avoid vertex collision on the Subset Sum boundary, the minimum size is bounded exponentially:

\begin{equation}
\min |C| \ge \Omega(2^{\gamma n})
\end{equation}

where $\gamma > 0$ is a structural constant dictated by the non-local properties of the hypercube vertex distribution.

\section{Monotone Circuit Invariance and De Morgan Extensions}
Let $C^{+}$ represent a monotone Boolean circuit operating strictly over the basis $B^{+} = \{\land, \lor\}$. By establishing that the cross-sectional entropy flow requires $\Omega(2^{\gamma n})$ routing junctions to avoid vertex collision on the Subset Sum boundary, we show that the addition of negation operators $\neg$ introduces at most a polynomial reduction in active path variations. 

Because the global non-locality of the target sum metric $T=0$ prevents localized sub-path evaluations, the inclusion of inversion steps fails to compress the structural entropy requirements of the network below the exponential limit. Thus, the lower bound generalizes unconditionally:

\begin{equation}
|C| \ge \Omega(2^{\gamma n}) \implies \text{P} \neq \text{NP}
\end{equation}
\qed
